Build Skills for Tomorrow

Are you looking to gain a new skill or advance in your career? Whether you are entering the workforce, changing careers, or seeking growth in your current field, there are resources available to help you succeed.

WIOA-Funded Training

The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) is a federally funded workforce development program that helps eligible job seekers access employment, education, occupational training, and support services needed to succeed in today's labor market.

Through TVCC's WIOA program, eligible participants may receive:

  • Scholarships for vocational training certificates in growth industries (examples include truck driving, medical assisting, project management, digital marketing, human resources management, accounting, data analytics, user experience (UX) design, and more)
  • Assistance paying for industry-recognized certification exams (such as PMP, NREMT, CompTIA A+, and CISSP)
  • Help paying for interview clothing and transportation to a new job or training
  • Free one-on-one career advising and job search assistance

Attend an Orientation or Career Pivoting and WIOA-Funded Training workshop to learn more about eligibility and available funding opportunities.

Learn more about our Career Services & Counseling or browse upcoming Workshops & Events.

Forklift & OSHA Safety Training

Join us for a FREE training program designed to prepare you for today's jobs in the warehousing and distribution industry.

Participants can become Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) and forklift certified at no cost. Applicants must be 18 years of age or older.

These training cohorts are offered periodically throughout the year and are promoted on our website and in our newsletter. Be sure to check back regularly for upcoming cohort announcements and registration opportunities.

Typing Tests

Need a certified typing test for employment?

TVCC administers certified typing tests at our Career Center.

Highlights include:

  • Five-minute certified typing test
  • Up to five attempts, with your highest score used for certification
  • Gross and net words per minute (WPM), keystrokes, errors, and accuracy reported
  • No appointment required
  • $15 certification fee (no cash accepted)

Learn more on our Typing Tests page.

Microsoft Office Training

Improve your skills in Microsoft Excel, Word, PowerPoint, and Outlook through TVCC's Microsoft Office Training program powered by TestGenius.

This self-paced program includes interactive learning modules that can be completed at your convenience with no time limit. Upon completion, you'll take an assessment to demonstrate competency and receive a certificate.

There is a one-time fee of $60, which provides access to all available modules.

To enroll, call TVCC at (925) 416-5100.

Apprenticeships

Apprenticeships combine paid employment with classroom instruction, allowing participants to earn while they learn.

Through the Chabot-Las Positas Community College District (CLPCCD) apprenticeship programs:

  • Apprentices receive free training.
  • Participants are employed from their first day.
  • Wages increase automatically as skills are gained.
  • Opportunities extend beyond the traditional construction trades into healthcare, manufacturing, government, and many other industries.
  • Approximately 92% of apprentices remain employed after completing their program.

Learn more at Apprenticeship4You.

Answers to Common Questions

Below are some of the most common questions we receive from job seekers about the benefits of working with the Tri-Valley Career Center (TVCC) and how to navigate the process. Since TVCC offers many resources, services, and opportunities, we can't cover everything here. If you don't find the answer you're looking for, please contact us—we're happy to discuss your specific situation and help you determine your next best steps.

Can you find me a job?

The Career Center is a multifaceted resource for job seekers, but we are not an employment agency.

While we offer a Job Board featuring local employment opportunities and maintain relationships with local and regional employers, we focus on helping you develop the skills, confidence, and connections needed to succeed in your job search. We can connect you with employers that match your skills and goals, but we do not place applicants into jobs or apply on your behalf.

What is WIOA?

The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) is the federal law that guides workforce development programs across the country and provides funding for many of the services offered at TVCC.

WIOA allows us to provide free employment and career services to job seekers and employers, while also offering additional assistance for eligible individuals returning to the workforce, including career counseling, training opportunities, and supportive services.

Learn more on our Training Opportunities page.

Do I need to enroll in WIOA to use TVCC?

No. Enrollment is not required to use many of TVCC's services.

Anyone may attend workshops, use many of our resources, and participate in a variety of programs without enrolling in WIOA.

However, because TVCC receives partial funding through WIOA, we are required to document services and outcomes. As a result, one-on-one Career Counseling is generally reserved for individuals enrolled in WIOA.

What do I get out of enrolling in WIOA?

TVCC offers several job search workshops each week that are open to everyone.

Individuals enrolled in WIOA may also be eligible for more intensive services at no cost, including:

  • One-on-one Career Counseling
  • Individualized job matching
  • Possible vocational training assistance
  • Additional career planning and support services

Who can enroll in WIOA?

While everyone is welcome to use the Career Center and attend workshops regardless of residency or age, general eligibility for WIOA enrollment includes:

  • Living or working in Alameda County (outside Oakland) within the past six months
  • Providing documentation showing authorization to work in the United States
  • Registration with the Selective Service System (for men age 18 and older, when applicable)

TVCC staff can help determine your eligibility and explain the enrollment process.

What is the first step?

We recommend beginning by visiting the Career Center and registering for one of our Orientation workshops.

During Orientation, you'll learn about:

  • TVCC services and resources
  • Workshops and Job Clubs
  • Community referrals and partner organizations
  • The WIOA program and its benefits
  • Next steps for your job search

After Orientation, we encourage you to attend our core strategy workshops and participate in a Job Club to build your skills, confidence, and professional network.

See upcoming Workshops & Events.

How much do your services cost?

With the exception of certain certifications—such as Typing Tests—TVCC's services are free to job seekers thanks to federal funding.

Learn more about available services on our Career Services & Counseling page.

I just lost my job. Where do I start?

Losing a job can be a life-changing experience, and it's normal to feel overwhelmed.

TVCC can help you through every stage of your job search by providing guidance with:

  • Job search strategies
  • Resume development
  • Interview preparation
  • Networking
  • Career planning
  • Finding immediate "survival jobs" while pursuing long-term career goals

Our goal is to help you build your skills, confidence, and network so you can move forward successfully.

I'm already working but want a new job. Can you help?

Absolutely.

TVCC serves job seekers regardless of their current employment status. Because many working professionals cannot visit during traditional business hours, we offer evening Job Clubs and workshops on Tuesdays and Wednesdays.

We also provide more than 20 downloadable Tip Sheets that you can use anytime.

Can you help pay for training?

In some cases, yes.

TVCC has a limited amount of Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) funding available to help eligible participants pay for certificate training programs that support career advancement.

Funding may be available for approved training providers listed on California's Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL), as well as selected adult schools and community colleges approved by TVCC staff.

Additional eligibility requirements apply. Attend a Career Pivoting and WIOA-Funded Training workshop or speak with a Career Counselor to learn more.

I worry my age may work against me. Can you help?

Yes.

TVCC offers an Issues of the "Older Worker" workshop that provides practical strategies for experienced professionals navigating today's job market.

Your knowledge, experience, and perspective are valuable assets. Our goal is to help you present those strengths effectively and confidently throughout your job search.

Can I use TVCC's space just to work on my search?

Absolutely.

We encourage job seekers to step away from the distractions of home and take advantage of the Career Center as a productive place to work.

Our workspace includes:

  • Computers
  • Wi-Fi
  • Internet access
  • Printing resources
  • Opportunities to network with other job seekers

You'll also have the chance to connect with people facing similar challenges while working toward the same goal—finding meaningful employment.

Expert Tax Help at No Cost

The Tri-Valley Career Center (TVCC) is committed to helping our community reduce the cost of tax preparation by offering free tax assistance through our Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) program. Our IRS-certified volunteers are competent, professional, and compassionate, helping eligible households prepare accurate tax returns and claim valuable tax credits such as the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC), Child Tax Credit (CTC), Education Credits, and California state tax credits.

Please note: We are not providing Individual Taxpayer Identification Numbers (ITINs) this year.

Who Qualifies

TVCC's free tax preparation services are intended for eligible taxpayers during the annual tax filing season.

Before scheduling an appointment, please review the current income eligibility guidelines to confirm that you qualify for the program.

During your appointment, you must provide:

  • Physical photo identification
  • Social Security cards or Individual Taxpayer Identification Numbers (ITINs) for everyone listed on the return
  • Copies of all tax documents

If you are married filing jointly, both you and your spouse must be present at the appointment.

Please review the "What to Bring With You" information before scheduling your appointment.

What to Bring

To help ensure your appointment goes smoothly, bring:

  • Government-issued photo identification
  • Social Security cards or ITIN documentation for every person listed on the return
  • All tax documents needed to prepare your return
  • Any additional documents requested when scheduling your appointment

Incomplete documentation may require you to schedule a new appointment before your return can be prepared.

Service Options

TVCC offers two convenient ways to have your tax return prepared.

In-Person Tax Preparation

One visit (approximately 1.5–3 hours)

  1. A Greeter will provide an intake sheet, or you may complete IRS Form 13614-C before your appointment and bring it with you.
  2. A Screener will review your intake form to ensure you have all required documents.
  3. An IRS-certified VITA Tax Preparer will prepare your return.
  4. A Quality Reviewer will verify your return for completeness and accuracy.
  5. You will receive a printed copy of your return and sign IRS Form 8879 authorizing electronic filing.
  6. Your return will be submitted electronically.

Drop-Off Service

Two visits (approximately 30 minutes each)

First Visit

  1. Complete or bring IRS Form 13614-C.
  2. Meet with a Screener to review your documents.
  3. Complete your intake interview and ask any questions.
  4. Leave your tax documents with our team for preparation.

Second Visit

  1. Meet with a Quality Reviewer to review your completed return.
  2. Receive a printed copy of your return.
  3. Sign IRS Form 8879 authorizing electronic filing.
  4. Your return will be submitted electronically.

Returns We Cannot Accommodate

The following returns are outside the scope of our Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) program and cannot be prepared at TVCC:

  • Married filing separately
  • Foreclosure or short sale of a personal residence
  • Rental property income
  • Sale of virtual currency
  • Self-employment or sole proprietor businesses with expenses over $25,000, business losses, depreciation, or home office deductions
  • Part-year California residents
  • Sale of stocks acquired through stock options
  • Kiddie Tax returns
  • Military tax returns

If your return falls into one of these categories, we recommend consulting a qualified tax professional.

Volunteer with Us

TVCC trains and supports a team of dedicated Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) volunteers who provide free tax preparation services to our community each year.

If you enjoy helping others and would like to make a meaningful impact during tax season, volunteering with the VITA program is a rewarding opportunity. Volunteers receive IRS training and certification before assisting taxpayers.

To learn more about volunteering, please contact the Tri-Valley Career Center.

Before scheduling:

  • Review the income eligibility requirements.
  • Review the "What to Bring" checklist.
  • Choose either an In-Person or Drop-Off appointment.

To schedule your appointment, use the appointment system during the annual tax preparation season or contact TVCC for assistance.

Build a Stronger Workforce

The Tri-Valley Career Center (TVCC) partners with employers to develop skilled, productive employees through workforce development programs, training resources, and financial incentives. Whether you're hiring new employees or investing in your current workforce, our training assistance programs can help your organization grow while reducing training costs.

On-the-Job Training (OJT)

On-the-Job Training (OJT) is a workforce development program that helps employers hire and train new employees while offsetting a portion of training costs.

Through OJT, eligible employers may receive reimbursement for a percentage of a new employee's wages during the training period while the employee learns the skills required for the position.

Benefits of OJT include:

  • Financial assistance for employee training
  • Customized, hands-on learning in your workplace
  • Increased employee retention
  • Access to qualified job candidates referred through TVCC

Employers must provide permanent, full-time employment opportunities (generally 32 or more hours per week), and positions must meet program eligibility requirements. Contact TVCC to determine whether your organization qualifies.

Customized Training

TVCC works with employers and workforce partners to identify training solutions that address specific business needs.

Customized Training may be available for employers who are:

  • Expanding their workforce
  • Introducing new technologies or equipment
  • Upskilling existing employees
  • Responding to changing industry requirements

Depending on the program and funding source, participating employers may receive reimbursement for a portion of eligible training costs.

Our staff will work with you to identify available workforce funding opportunities and determine the best training solution for your organization.

OSHA Safety Training

The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) Training Institute Education Center at the Chabot-Las Positas Community College District (CLPCCD) provides nationally recognized workplace safety and health training for employers, supervisors, and employees.

Courses are available in topics such as:

  • Construction safety
  • General industry safety
  • Hazard communication
  • Fall protection
  • Confined spaces
  • Electrical safety
  • OSHA 10-Hour and OSHA 30-Hour courses
  • Professional certificate programs

Training can help organizations improve workplace safety, reduce injuries, support regulatory compliance, and strengthen employee knowledge.

Learn more about available courses through the CLPCCD OSHA Training Institute Education Center.

Apprenticeship Programs

Apprenticeships combine paid employment with classroom instruction to develop highly skilled workers.

Employers who participate in apprenticeship programs benefit by:

  • Building a reliable talent pipeline
  • Developing employees with skills tailored to their business
  • Improving employee retention
  • Reducing recruitment costs
  • Strengthening succession planning

Apprentices earn wages while learning and receive progressively higher pay as they gain experience and complete training milestones.

Through CLPCCD's apprenticeship programs, opportunities are available in construction, manufacturing, healthcare, government, transportation, and many other industries.

Volunteer opportunities vary throughout the year and may be available on a recurring or seasonal basis.

Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA)

One of TVCC's largest volunteer programs is the annual Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) program.

IRS-certified volunteers help eligible community members prepare and electronically file federal and California tax returns at no cost.

Volunteers receive training and certification before assisting taxpayers and work as part of a supportive team dedicated to serving the community.

No prior tax preparation experience is required for many volunteer roles.

Learn more about our Free Tax Preparation program.

Serving the Tri-Valley Community

For more than 45 years, the Tri-Valley Career Center (TVCC) has connected job seekers, employers, and community partners with the resources they need to succeed. Through career services, workforce development programs, employer support, and strategic partnerships, TVCC helps strengthen the regional workforce while supporting economic opportunity throughout the Tri-Valley and greater Alameda County.

Our Approach

TVCC's mission is to empower job seekers, businesses, and communities to thrive by providing innovative employment services and workforce solutions.

We believe successful workforce development is built on collaboration. By partnering with employers, educational institutions, public agencies, and community organizations, we help individuals prepare for meaningful careers while supporting businesses in finding and developing qualified employees.

Our services include:

  • Career counseling and job search assistance
  • Employment workshops and Job Clubs
  • Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) employment and training services
  • Employer recruitment and hiring support
  • Job fairs and hiring events
  • Workforce training and apprenticeship resources
  • Community referrals and supportive services

Funding & Affiliations

TVCC is supported through a combination of federal, state, and local workforce development funding and community partnerships.

Many of our employment and training services are funded through the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA), federal legislation that supports workforce development programs across the United States. WIOA funding allows eligible job seekers to access career counseling, employment assistance, occupational training, and supportive services at little or no cost.

TVCC also collaborates with regional workforce agencies, educational institutions, employers, and community organizations to expand access to employment opportunities and workforce resources.

Chabot-Las Positas Community College District

TVCC is part of the Chabot-Las Positas Community College District (CLPCCD) Economic Development Department.

CLPCCD serves communities throughout the East Bay by providing higher education, workforce training, contract education, apprenticeships, Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) training, and economic development initiatives that support students, employers, and regional industries.

This connection enables TVCC to link job seekers and employers with a broader network of educational and workforce development resources.

Learn more at Chabot-Las Positas Community College District.

Alameda County Workforce Development Board

TVCC works closely with the Alameda County Workforce Development Board (ACWDB) to deliver workforce programs that help residents prepare for employment while supporting employers with recruitment, training, and business services.

The partnership helps ensure that workforce investments align with regional labor market needs and provides access to valuable employment, training, and business resources.

Learn more at Alameda County Workforce Development Board.

EastBay Works

TVCC is a proud partner of EastBay Works, a regional network of workforce development organizations serving employers and job seekers throughout Alameda County.

EastBay Works connects individuals to employment services, training opportunities, career centers, and workforce resources while helping businesses recruit qualified employees and strengthen their workforce.

Learn more at EastBay Works.
