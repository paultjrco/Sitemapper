Mission Statement:

We empower individuals and communities to thrive by creating career, education, and economic opportunities through innovative and effective occupational safety and health training. As an OSHA-authorized training center, we are committed to enhancing workplace safety culture and performance by providing high-quality training that leads to Continuing Education Units (CEUs) and supports lifelong learning and professional growth.

Vision Statement:

The OSHA Training Institute Education Centers (OTIEC) will be the premier network leading occupational safety and health training across the country, setting the standard for excellence in safety education and workforce development.

The Chabot-Las Positas Community College District (CLPCCD) OTIEC is authorized by OSHA to deliver occupational safety and health training on behalf of OSHA and has been conducting OSHA’s courses since 2008. Courses offered include OSHA safety standards, OSHA Outreach Trainer courses, OSHA Seminars, environmental courses, and Cal/OSHA courses. The OSHA Training Institute Education Center specializes in delivering safety training courses in construction, maritime, disaster site and general industry throughout California, Oregon, Washington, Alaska, Idaho, Nevada, Arizona, American Samoa, Hawaii, Guam, Northern Mariana Islands, and at your facility.

Chabot-Las Positas Community College District operates the only authorized OSHA Training Institute Education Center headquartered in Northern California. Our instructors are industry safety professionals who bring the latest information, experience and techniques to our state-of-the-art classrooms and incorporate real world experiences into classroom instruction and hands-on learning exercises.

Our primary goal is to fulfill OSHA’s mission of preventing work-related injuries, illnesses and fatalities by offering the finest quality programs and courses. Students are able to earn Continuing Education Units (CEUs) which can be applied to many safety professional designations, including certifications offered by the Board of Certified Safety Professionals (BCSP), American Board of Industrial Hygiene and others.

Students come from around the globe and value the quality of instruction, affordable costs, friendly and competent staff, knowledgeable instructors and all of the added benefits that adult learners experience from attending our courses. Our training locations are centrally located, easily accessible and are all within driving distance of scenic recreation areas and parks, dining, shopping and a wide variety of entertainment and nightlife.

For additional information about the OSHA Training Institute Education Center, our upcoming courses , or customized on-site OSHA trainings at your location, contact us at 866-936-6742 .

Learn More About Our Online Safety Training.

The OSHA Training Institute Education Center at Chabot-Las Positas Community College District offers flexible online safety training for workers and employers. Our courses help organizations strengthen workplace safety, reduce risk, and meet important training needs.

Online learning also makes safety education easier to manage. Employers can assign courses based on job duties, work locations, industry requirements, or company goals. As a result, organizations can provide consistent training across teams and locations. Employees can build useful knowledge without leaving the workplace for every course.

In addition, our online safety training options support both individual learners and larger organizations. Courses cover a wide range of workplace safety and health topics. Therefore, employers can select training that matches the needs of their workforce.

For employees who need OSHA 10-hour or OSHA 30-hour training, our OTIEC offers actively proctored online options. These courses are delivered through an OSHA-authorized online training provider. Consequently, students can feel confident in the quality, oversight, and compliance of the training.

Online courses also provide valuable flexibility. Participants can complete training from work, home, or another convenient location. They can also access courses at times that fit their schedules. General Information

What types of 10- and 30-hour classes are available?

OSHA offers outreach training program classes for the following industries: Construction, General Industry, Maritime, and Disaster Site work.

Does OSHA require the 10- and 30-hour outreach classes?

No, they are voluntary. OSHA recommends these classes as an orientation to occupational safety and health for workers/supervisors. However, some states have enacted laws mandating the training. Also, some employers, unions, organizations or other jurisdictions may also require this training.

How do I find a trainer?

OSHA authorized outreach trainers are not OSHA personnel; trainers are authorized (not certified) through our program to deliver outreach training classes. Since outreach trainers are independent service providers, their schedules and fees may vary.

OSHA has a searchable outreach trainer list that may be used to find a trainer nearest to you. You can also contact our office for additional assistance.

Please note that OSHA authorized outreach trainers are not OSHA personnel; trainers are authorized (not certified) through our program to deliver outreach training classes. Since outreach trainers are independent service providers, their schedules and fees may vary.

Do 10- and 30-hour cards expire?

The student course completion cards (10- and 30-hour cards) in all industries do not expire. The form and content of additional training is left to the discretion of the student and/or employer. The Outreach Training Program is intended to be an orientation to Occupational Safety and Health; workers must receive additional training on specific hazards of their job.

How do I verify a 10- or 30-hour card?

Most cards issued after March 2016 (plastic cards) will have a QR code on the backside that can be scanned to verify authenticity. Paper cards can be verified by contacting the outreach trainer who issued the card. Please note that outreach trainers is required to maintain records only for training that took place within the previous 5 years.

How do I verify an authorized outreach trainer card?

Cards issued after March 2016 (plastic cards) will have a QR code on the backside that can be scanned to verify authenticity. You can also contact our office for verification.

How do I report suspected fraudulent cards/activity?

Please contact OSHA’s Outreach Training Program at [email protected] or call the outreach fraud hotline at (847) 725-7804 to file complaints about card or program fraud and abuse. Provide all pertinent information, including the trainer’s name, the card issuing date, the location of training, the type of training (e.g. construction, general industry) and whether it is a 10 or 30-hour card. Include any other relevant information including your name and contact information. OSHA will maintain the confidentiality of the person reporting the information.

How do I get a replacement card?

If you lost or misplaced your student course completion card, you must contact your trainer for a replacement. Your trainer will then contact their Authorizing Training Organization (ATO) to get a replacement card made.

Please note:

  • Replacement cards will not be issued if the original training took place more than five (5) years ago.
  • You may only receive one (1) replacement card. You will need to retake the class if your card is lost or misplaced again.

If I have other questions, who can I contact?

You can contact us by calling 866-936-6742 or contact us online .

Outreach Trainer Questions

When does my trainer card expire?

Trainer cards expire 4 years after the issuing date. Your expiration date will be listed on your outreach trainer card or you can contact us to confirm.

To maintain your authorization status, you must take an update course (e.g. OSHA 502, 503, etc.) before you expire. We strongly recommend trainers schedule their refresher training well in advance of their expiration date to avoid a lapse in authorization.

As of January 1, 2019, there is no grace period after you expire. If your card has expired, you will need to meet all prerequisites requirements to attend the initial Trainer course (e.g. OSHA 500, 501, etc.) again, including having successfully completed the OSHA Standards course (e.g. OSHA 510, 511, etc.) within the last 7 calendar years. Extensions will not be granted.

If a student misses a portion of class, can I give them makeup training?

If the student completed at least 50% of the original class, they are eligible to receive makeup training. Makeup training must be completed within six (6) months of the start of the initial training course. Makeup training classes consisting of fewer than three (3) students are permitted.

Please contact our office at 866-936-6742 or contact us online or refer to the Outreach Training Program Requirements document for more information.

Can I issue student course completion cards outside of the U.S.?

OSHA training programs are intended for workers within OSHA’s geographical jurisdiction. These locations include all 50 U.S. States, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, American Samoa, Guam, the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, Wake Island, Outer Continental Shelf Lands defined in the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act, and Johnston Island. For this reason, OSHA Outreach Training Program classes must be limited to these areas.

The only exception is for OSHA Outreach Training Program classes delivered to members of the U.S. military and to civil service employees (Federal Competitive Service, Exception Service, or Senior Executive Service employees only) outside of the geographical jurisdiction. Issuing student course completion cards in this situation is allowable but you must notify our office before submitting a card request.

  1. Transfers from one class into another class are not allowed.

Virtual, Instructor-Led Classes via Zoom

Our Virtual Instructor-Led Training (VILT) classes are conducted in a live-video delivery format via Zoom.

Participation Requirements:

  1. The class will be conducted using ZOOM . In order to participate, you must have:
    • Broadband internet connection
    • Desktop or laptop computer or a tablet, microphone and webcam. Test your internet connection in advance at **https://zoom.us/test** .
  2. Attendance in the entire class – all session(s) – is required for successful completion.
  3. Review “ Zoom Best Practices For Attendees ” and “ **Zoom How-To Guide** .”

Course Materials:

Student materials will be sent electronically unless otherwise requested at least 5 days prior to start date – to allow for ground shipping.

Access:

You will receive an invitation to your course in Canvas after enrolling. Complete the pre-course instructions to register, and receive the link, for your Zoom course.​
